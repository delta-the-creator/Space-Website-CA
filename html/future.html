The Future

This page is dedicated for the exciting future events happening in Space Exploration.

SPHEREx, No Earlier than February 27, 2025

The Spectro-Photometer for the History of the Universe, Epoch of Reionisation and Ices Explorer. This observatory will collect data on more than 450 million galaxies, and also 100 million stars in the Milky Way.

Axiom Mission 4, No Earlier than Spring 2025

The Axiom 4 mission will launch aboard a SpaceX Dragon spacecraft and travel to the International Space Station. The crew will dock and spend 14 days on the orbiting laboratory, conducting experiments for science and other commercial businesses.

Artemis III Mission in 2027

Artemis III astronauts will travel to lunar orbit, where two crew members will descend to the surface, and spend approximately a week near the South Pole of the Moon. Astronauts will be conducting more experiments to do with the rocks on the moon.

CLPS Flight: Intuitive Machines in 2027

Intuitive Machines' forth awarded flight will deliver six NASA payloads to part of the Moon where nighttime temperatures are low, and places where are shadowed or are with rugged terrain.

DAVINCI in 2030

The DAVINCI mission (Deep Atmosphere Venus Investigation of Noble gases, Chemistry, and Imaging) will study Venus from it's Atmosphere to the planet's surface.
